Seven Foundations

Understanding how change actually works

I – You Aren't The Person Yet

You focus on changing actions. You should focus on changing identity.

When it comes to setting big goals, people focus on changing their actions to make progress. But that's second order.

First order: Changing who you are so that your behavior naturally follows.

The bodybuilder doesn't "grind" to eat healthy. They can't see themselves living any other way. The CEO doesn't discipline themselves to show up. They would hate lying in bed past their alarm.

If you want a specific outcome in life, you must have the lifestyle that creates that outcome long before you reach it.

When you truly change yourself, all of your old habits become disgusting, because you have a deep awareness of what kind of life those actions compound into.

II – You Don't Actually Want It

Your actions reveal your true goals, not your words.

"Trust only movement. Life happens at the level of events, not of words." - Alfred Adler

All behavior is goal-oriented. It's teleological. But most of your goals are unconscious.

If you can't stop procrastinating, you're not lacking discipline. You're achieving the goal of protecting yourself from judgment that comes from finishing and sharing your work.

If you stay in a dead-end job, you're not lacking courage. You're pursuing the goal of safety, predictability, and avoiding the appearance of failure.

Real change requires changing your goals. Not setting new ones, but changing your point of view.

A goal is a projection into the future that acts as a lens of perception, allowing you to notice information, ideas, and resources that aid in achieving it.

III – You're Afraid To Be There

Your identity is defended like physical survival.

The Anatomy of Identity:

  1. You want to achieve a goal
  2. You perceive reality through the lens of that goal
  3. You notice "important" information (learning)
  4. You act toward that goal and receive feedback
  5. You repeat until it becomes automatic (conditioning)
  6. That behavior becomes identity ("I am the type of person who...")
  7. You defend your identity to maintain psychological consistency
  8. Your identity shapes new goals, restarting the cycle

This process started when you were a child. You were conditioned through reward and punishment to adopt beliefs and values that kept you safe.

When your identity feels threatened, you go into fight or flight. The same stress response as physical danger.

If you're heavily identified with a political ideology, religion, profession, or lifestyle, challenges to those beliefs feel like attacks on your survival.

IV – Your Life Exists At A Level of Mind

The mind evolves through 9 predictable stages.

9 Stages of Ego Development:

  1. Impulsive - No separation between impulse and action
  2. Self-Protective - The world is dangerous, look out for yourself
  3. Conformist - You are your group and its rules
  4. Self-Aware - You notice an inner life that doesn't match the exterior
  5. Conscientious - You build your own system of principles
  6. Individualist - You see your principles were shaped by context
  7. Strategist - You work with systems while aware of your involvement
  8. Construct-Aware - You see all frameworks as useful fictions
  9. Unitive - Separation between self and life dissolves

Most readers hover between stages 4-8. Moving through them follows a pattern you can navigate consciously.

V – Intelligence Is Getting What You Want

Success requires agency, opportunity, and intelligence.

The Formula for Success:

  • Agency - Your likelihood to act toward goals
  • Opportunity - Goals that bear fruit
  • Intelligence - Your ability to iterate and learn

Cybernetics (from Greek "to steer") illustrates the properties of intelligent systems:

  1. Have a goal
  2. Act toward that goal
  3. Sense where you are
  4. Compare it to the goal
  5. Act again based on feedback

High intelligence is the ability to iterate, persist, and understand the big picture. Low intelligence is the inability to learn from mistakes.

The reality is that you can achieve any goal you set your mind to. There is a series of choices that lead to any outcome. You may not have the resources yet, but intelligence means knowing they can be acquired.

VI – The Three Phases of Transformation

Identity flips happen fast after tension builds.

When people successfully flip their identity, it follows this pattern:

  1. Dissonance - They feel like they don't belong in their current life and become sufficiently fed up
  2. Uncertainty - They don't know what comes next, so they experiment or get lost
  3. Discovery - They discover what they want and make 6 years of progress in 6 months

Our goal: reach dissonance, navigate uncertainty, and discover what you truly want with overwhelming clarity.

VII – Turn Your Life Into A Video Game

Games create obsession through structure.

Games are the poster child for obsession, enjoyment, and flow states. They have all the components that lead to focus and clarity.

The 6 Components:

  • Anti-vision (What's at stake) - The life you never want to experience
  • Vision (How you win) - The ideal life you're building toward
  • 1 Year Goal (The mission) - Your sole priority
  • 1 Month Project (Boss fight) - How you gain XP and loot
  • Daily Levers (Quests) - The daily process that unlocks opportunities
  • Constraints (Rules) - Limitations that encourage creativity

These act as concentric circles - a forcefield - that guard your mind from distractions and shiny objects.
